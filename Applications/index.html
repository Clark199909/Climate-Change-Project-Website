Applications Introduction

Earth Orbit Application

In this application, students will:

  • explore the three orbital parameters (eccentricity, obliquity, and precession) and their influences on insolation;
  • identify patterns of change in these parameters' graphs;
  • understand the importance of time ranges when analyzing or comparing data;

Water Cycle Application

In this application, students will:

  • learn about the concepts of delta 18O and oxygen isotopes;
  • compare how water molecules move and delta 18O change in glacial and inter-glacial/non-glacial periods;
  • understand why we would need to look at delta 18O to know when ice ages occur;

Delta 18O and Insolation Data Application

In this application, students will:

  • know what upward and downwrad trends in delta 18O data mean to ice volume;
  • identify the "present" in the graph and think about what the data of the "present" indicates;
  • compare the curves of insolation and delta 18O to learn about the correlation between these two things;
